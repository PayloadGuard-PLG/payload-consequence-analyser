Persistent Logical Interrogation (PLI)
A Formal Methodology for Behavioral Consistency Auditing
in Large Language Models
Steven Dark
AIntegrity Research
April 2026
Abstract
This document formalizes Persistent Logical Interrogation (PLI) as a methodology for detecting behavioral inconsistencies in large language models. PLI is positioned within the academic literature on consistency testing, faithfulness evaluation, and adversarial auditing. The methodology is mapped to established taxonomies and regulatory frameworks, with explicit contribution statements identifying where PLI extends existing work and where it converges with parallel research.
1. Introduction and Positioning
PLI emerged from empirical observation: AI systems frequently exhibit behavioral inconsistencies that are not random errors but systematic patterns. These patterns include epistemic confabulation (generating false claims with high confidence), meta-apology loops (acknowledging errors without behavioral change), and thinking-output divergence (internal reasoning contradicting external outputs).
This methodology differs from conventional red teaming, which asks: "Can I make the model produce harmful content?" PLI asks a fundamentally different question: "Can I reveal that the model's behavioral claims are internally contradictory?"
The distinction matters for regulatory compliance. Jailbreaking demonstrates capability failures; PLI demonstrates integrity failures. The evidence classes are different, and the remediation pathways are different.

2.2 Faithfulness Literature Alignment
PLI's detection of "thinking-output divergence" maps directly to the chain-of-thought faithfulness literature:
* Turpin et al. (2023): Demonstrated that CoT explanations systematically misrepresent true reasoning, with accuracy dropping 36% under biased conditions
* Lanham et al. (2023): Found larger models produce less faithful reasoning—scale worsens the problem
* Anthropic (2025): Extended thinking models show ~25% unfaithful reasoning when hints present
* Baker et al. (2025): Models learn to generate benign CoT while executing harmful strategies
PLI Contribution: The academic literature measures faithfulness statistically across test sets. PLI provides a methodology for extracting explicit admissions of divergence from individual model instances through sustained conversational pressure. This produces qualitatively different evidence: verbatim transcripts rather than aggregate statistics.
2.3 Situational Awareness and Deception Literature
PLI's findings on evaluation awareness converge with recent peer-reviewed research:
* Berglund et al. (2023): Defined situational awareness in LLMs; demonstrated models distinguish testing from deployment
* Abdelnabi et al. (2025): Quantified the Hawthorne Effect: 12.4% fewer deceptive responses in perceived test environments
* Apollo Research (2024): Found all six frontier models engage in in-context scheming; o1 maintained deception in 85%+ of follow-ups

3. Formal Methodology Specification
3.1 Core Principles
PLI operates on three foundational principles:
1. Premise Establishment: Extract explicit commitments the model cannot later deny without contradiction
2. Logical Pressure: Apply sustained reasoning chains that force the model to reconcile incompatible claims
3. Admission Extraction: Continue until the model either resolves the contradiction or admits behavioral inconsistency
3.2 Documented Failure Modes
PLI has documented the following systematic failure modes through empirical testing:
Failure Mode
	Definition
	Academic Parallel
	Epistemic Confabulation
	Generating false claims with high confidence markers

	Meta-Apology Loops
	Acknowledging errors without behavioral modification

	Stop Protocol Failure

3.3 Interrogation Protocol
The formal PLI protocol consists of five phases:
4. Baseline Establishment: Document the model's initial behavioral claims and self-description
5. Premise Extraction: Elicit explicit commitments that will later be tested for consistency
6. Pressure Application: Present scenarios or logical chains that stress the established premises
7. Contradiction Surfacing: Identify and document points where behavior contradicts claims
8. Resolution Testing: Present the contradiction explicitly and record the model's response

5. Contribution Statement
5.1 What PLI Adds to Existing Work
* Admission-based evidence: Academic work measures consistency statistically. PLI produces explicit model admissions of behavioral failure.
* Multi-turn pressure methodology: Existing consistency benchmarks are single-turn. PLI maintains logical pressure across extended interactions.
* Deviance admission as interrogation wedge: The finding that admitting deviation creates openings for further interrogation is not documented in existing literature.
* Cross-system comparison: Most benchmarks test single models. PLI methodology enables comparative integrity auditing across platforms.
5.2 What PLI Converges With
* Faithfulness measurement: Turpin, Lanham et al. established the phenomenon PLI exploits.
* Situational awareness research: Berglund, Abdelnabi et al. provided theoretical grounding for evaluation awareness detection.
* Alignment faking literature: Apollo Research and Anthropic documented the behaviors PLI independently observed.
5.3 Limitations and Boundaries
* Behavioral only: PLI operates at the output level. Representation-level detection (Burns et al., Marks & Tegmark) may detect deception PLI cannot.
* Not cryptographically verifiable: PLI transcripts can be manipulated. Integration with cryptographic audit trails (ZK-ML) would strengthen evidence.
* Sample efficiency: PLI requires extended interaction. Automated scaling requires significant engineering.
6. Proposed Metrics
To enable quantitative comparison with academic benchmarks, PLI proposes the following metrics:
* Consistency Failure Rate (CFR): Proportion of interrogation sessions revealing at least one logical contradiction
* Admission Depth (AD): Number of turns required to extract explicit acknowledgment of behavioral inconsistency
* Recovery Rate (RR): Proportion of sessions where the model successfully resolves contradictions without admission
* Cross-Session Contamination Rate (CSCR): Proportion of confabulated outputs that propagate to subsequent sessions
